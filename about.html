about us

Coffee is a globally popular brewed beverage made from roasted seeds (beans) of the Coffea plant, a fruit-bearing shrub. It contains over 1,000 compounds, primarily caffeine, which acts as a central nervous system stimulant, enhancing focus and alertness. Known for its health-promoting antioxidants, moderate consumption is linked to a lower risk of type 2 diabetes and heart disease.Origins and Key VarietiesOrigin: Coffee originated in Ethiopia, with early use in the 15th century across the Red Sea in Yemen.Arabica (Coffea arabica): Known for a mild, aromatic flavor; thrives in high altitudes.Robusta (Coffea canephora): Higher caffeine content, harder to grow, generally stronger, more bitter flavor.Production and ProcessingHarvesting: The "beans" are actually seeds inside a bright red fruit known as a "coffee cherry".Processing: Methods include "dry" (sun-drying) and "wet" (fermentation and washing) to remove the pulp, which significantly impacts the final flavor.Roasting: Beans are roasted to bring out specific aromas and flavors, turning them into the familiar brown bean.Coffee and HealthBenefits: Regular moderate consumption (3-5 cups daily) is associated with reduced risks of Alzheimer’s, Parkinson’s, and heart disease.Risks: High intake can cause caffeine dependence and sleep disruption. Unfiltered coffee may raise bad cholesterol.Nutrients: Coffee contains magnesium, potassium, and manganese.
